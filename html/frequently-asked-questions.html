  • + Please explain the charge off and tell me how can I fix or remove these from my credit report?

    How to Remove and Fix a Charge Off

    Usually, if an account remains unpaid for 180 or more days, the creditor will write off the issued debt as a loss. This is called a charge off. Once a debt is labelled as a charge off, the creditor will either transfer it to an in-house collection unit or it will be outsourced to a third-party that will attempt to collect this debt from you.

    You can not only receive annoying emails and phone calls from the collectors but also have a bad impact on your credit report. This charge off will not only be a part of your credit file for seven years but also impact your credit score poorly. If you have a bad credit score, your current lenders will increase your interest rate which will result in a higher repayment and more money spent over the years. Additionally, new creditors will be much more reluctant to lend you money if you have a charge off on your credit profile. Keeping these harsh truths in mind, you can get a charge off removed from your credit profile. Do the following:

    • Review and validate. You can suffer huge blows from inaccurate reporting. Get copies of your credit reports from all of the three bureaus to review the charge off details. Following are the main things that creditors are bound to prove in writing:
      • Initial agreement that you and the creditor made.
      • Transferring the debt ownership to any third party (if applicable)
      • The current account balance
      • Any penalties and fees added to your account
      • Collection agency’s right to pursue you regarding the debt in your current state of residence.
      Collection agency or your creditor has 30 days in which they need to validate your debts. If they are unable to do so, the debt has to be removed from your credit profile. If they are able to prove it, you still have some other options:
    • Strike a bargain. You might be able to strike a deal with your creditors if you bargain to pay a partial or the full amount of the debt. Before agreeing to do so, ask your creditors if they will remove the citations from your report. Ask for a written agreement that states the removal of citations from your credit profile. You should track each and every step you take and when you receive the letter, submit it to all three of the bureaus. In case, you are using the services of any credit repairing expert, please inform them about all the new developments.
    • Wait for the current citation to expire. In case your creditors don’t agree to remove the citations, paying off the debt might not be the most optimal strategy. “Re-aging” happens when the date of your last derogatory notation is updated on your credit report. When you decide to repay an older loan, this will reactivate your debt on the credit profile and this can result in unwanted damage. In this scenario, it is very important that you weigh the pros and cons of debt repayment and proceed accordingly.

    A charge off is delicate and complicated by nature. The right course of action depends entirely on your situation. A credit repair expert comes in very handy in these situations especially when debt collectors are involved. That's where wefixyourcreditreport.com shines. We are here to help you in your struggles with charge offs and find you a solution that is optimal and benefits you the most.

  • + Understanding the Fair Credit Reporting Act and why it was created?

    The Fair Credit Reporting Act or more commonly known as FCRA was designed in 1970 as an accessory to the Consumer Credit Protection Act. The FCRA provides extra measures to protect consumers in the areas of accuracy, fairness as well as privacy of their information when data is collected by credit bureaus. Additionally, it gives you the right to personally engage in the credit maintenance and repairing process. You have the right to verify all the information present on your credit report.

    Under the FCRA, federal consumer rights include:

    • Accessing your personal credit report. It is your right to learn about your credit profile. TansUnion, Equifax and Experian are bound under the FCRA to give you a free copy of your credit report every year. You can also get a free copy of your report if:
      • Any potential lender needs to views it
      • You have suffered identity theft or need to put a fraud alert on your profile.
      • You are currently unemployed and need to apply for a new job in the next 60
        • days.
      Visit annualcreditreport.com to a free copy of your credit report.
    • Accessing your credit score. Your credit score tells lenders the likelihood of you repaying the loan. Based on the numbers on your credit profile, banks, businesses and insurance companies decide whether to lend you money or not. You can request the credit bureaus to provide you with your credit report but it will cost you a small fee. If, however, you decide to avail our services, you can receive your updated credit score every 90 days.
    • Notification of instances when your credit data is used to harm you. In case your application for mortgage, auto loan, insurance coverage or any other credit is denied, the lender is bound to inform you about this. They are also bound by the law to give you the contact information of the respective credit bureaus that gave the said lendor your credit report.
    • Your right to file a dispute against information in your credit profile. Incomplete or false information can drag your credit score down. In addition to viewing your credit report, the FCRA allows you to trigger an investigation with the credit bureaus if you spot an error. Once the information is verified as false or inaccurate, it must be deleted or corrected within 30 days. Keep in mind that our service demands that creditors and bureaus uphold two additional reporting standards pursuant to other applicable laws -fairness and substantiation - in addition to simple accuracy.
    • Your right to get outdated information deleted. Negative items such as bankruptcy and collections can stay on your credit report for 7 to 10 years. After the said time, credit bureaus are bound to remove these negative items from your profile. Something to note here is the fact that there is no minimum limit for which these items have to stay on your credit profile so technically they can be removed earlier.
    • Your right to decide who can access your credit report. No one has the right to see your credit profile without your permissions. According to the FCRA, lenders, employers, landlords and any other concerned parties must get your contest in case they want to access your credit report.
    • You can choose to decline unsolicited offers. In case, you don’t want to receive any insurance coverage and pre-approved credit applications, you can take legal action. Any business that sends you these offers is also required to give you access to a toll free number so you can unsubscribe from any offer you want.
    • You can take legal action. A credit report is a very important aspect of your life and the FCRA restricts anyone else from misusing it. To get further information, you can avail the services of a professional credit fixing expert or you can visit the website of Federal Trade Commission at www.ftc.gov/credit.

  • + How Long Will Negative Items Stay on My Credit Reports?


    A consumer’s credit score is lowered by the past financial blunders and lending choices. Credit bureaus keep record of these negative items so that can determine your financial reliability and the likelihood of debt repayment.

    Negatives items usually stay for a maximum of 7 years on your credit profile whereas bankruptcies stay for 10 years. Hard inquiries tend to stay for two years and some types of student loans can stay forever.

    Negative items on your credit profile are not a good thing but there are ways in which you can make your situation better. Listed below are the negative items that can impact your credit profile and the ways in which you can remove these items from your profile.

    Hard Inquiries: Two Years

    Hard inquiries usually remain a part of your credit profile for two years. It is very difficult to avoid these because they usually occur when lenders will run your credit for mortgage, credit cards, car loans and other loan applications. You can minimize hard inquiries by doing your due research beforehand and you should apply to one lender at a time instead of multiple applications.

    When looking for a credit card, try to research for the best credit card that is suited for your needs. Only apply if you are sure that you will qualify.There is an upside though, when you are comparing rates between different lenders within a specific timeframe, multiple inquiries will be counted as one. This timeframe ranges between 14 and 45 days depending on the model of credit scoring that is used.

    Late Payments, Collection Accounts and More: Seven Years

    Following items might stay on the record for up to 7 years.

    • Late payments
    • Collection accounts
    • Filing for chapter 7 bankruptcy
    • Charge offs
    • Student loan default
    • Closed delinquent accounts

    Some of the negative items impact your credit profile more badly than the others. For example, older negative items such as late payments can be remedied by years of exceptional and timely repayment. If you have a lot of negative items on your credit profile that are 6 year old but since then you have made timely payments, you can have a great credit score. Your past can be remedied by your present. In such a case, lenders will be more inclined to offer you a loan.

    Repairing your credit can minimize or even eliminate the impact of these things. Something’s might even be wrongly added to your profile. A lender might not be able to substantiate your account. Look at the example of charge offs. A creditor is bound to show things such as your current balance, your payment history and the initial agreement between you and the lender in case a collection agency is involved. If all of these standards are not met and verified you are entitled to the removal of charge offs from your credit profile.


    Chapter 7 Bankruptcy: 10 Years

    If you have filed for a chapter 7 bankruptcy, it can stay on your profile for 10 years. Though most of the individual cases linked with bankruptcy (let’s say a delinquent car loan) will most probably disappear from your profile in 7 years. Credit repair process makes sure that the facts regarding the bankruptcy are reported properly and all the accounts associated with it are treated accordingly.


    Unpaid Perkins Federal Student Loans: Forever

    These loans might never get removed from your credit profile. The reason behind this is the fact that the money you have borrowed came from the government. Additionally, you can’t get rid of these loans even by declaring bankruptcy. If you have a private unpaid loan, it might be eliminated after 7 years.


    To Sum it Up

    The information sent by the creditors to the credit bureaus is not always accurate and can be associated with the wrong accounts.
