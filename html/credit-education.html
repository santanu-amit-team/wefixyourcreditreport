What is a credit report?

Q:
What's on my credit report?
A:
A credit report consists of data like your address and name, your loan history, account name, credit history, credit limits, credit inquiries, collections, public record, any late payments and of course, your current credit score. So basically a credit report indicates your complete financial history

Q:
How do things enter my credit report?
A:
Creditors such as credit card issuers, banks, cell phone service providers and hospitals will report your payment history to the relevant credit bureaus. Bureaus such as Equifax, TransUnion and Experian keep a tidy record of everything that is reported to them by creditors. This information includes the times when you paid your bills late. It also includes things like filing for bankruptcy and repayment of loans. Negative items will obviously impact your credit report negatively. No surprise there.

How can my credit improve?

Remember the following 5 factors we talked about earlier?

  • Work hard to remove any negative and questionable repayment history from your current credit report. (This is where we shine!)

  • Be older in age (Unfortunately, you have no control here but this is true, a longer history equals better score)
  • Do your best to get any hard and inaccurate inquiries completed erased from your credit report
  • Have a diverse credit by having a mix of installments, revolving and some other types of accounts.
